Mountain Fog, 2018

This piece is 12” x 12” and drawn with graphite. I used a picture I had taken outside of Red Lodge, Montana as a reference and manually scaled the image up in the drawing. While there are a lot of details in the three trees in the foreground, I wanted to keep the background details fairly simple. I kept the fog on the backmost mountain simple and let it fade into the gray, cloudy sky.





Balance, 2020

Balance is the first piece I created entirely with charcoal. This abstract piece is 21½” x 15½” and allowed me to get a feel for how charcoal worked and felt. I wanted to create a piece that had flow and lots of contrast. I added the curving line in the center with dark bold strokes and framed it with highlights using an eraser.





Stars in the Sky, 2020

Stars in the Sky is a 21½” x 15½” piece done entirely in charcoal. I originally made a collage on 8" x 11" paper with Van Gogh's Starry Night and other images I found. I kept the sky and layout of Starry Night so the viewer could still recognize it.
